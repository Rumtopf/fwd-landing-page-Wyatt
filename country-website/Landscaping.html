Landscaping

Landscaping on an acre of land which was mostly a hill was a consistent challenge against nature. large trees provided a lot of shady area's. so gardnening had to happen around where the sun would peak through The home itself was built on black granite rock that was blasted and duumped all over the yard, parts of which you will see in the gallery pictures.

The Coy Pond!

The pond, nestled between the house and garage, was built into the old driveway with pickaxe, shovel wheelbarrow and a just a little bit of hardwork. The proccess took a few months but as you can see what we got out of it was well worth it!

The Rockery!

The rockery or rockeries as we had two they were called the upper rockery and lower rockery. I have moved most of those rocks by hand my self. Black granite is a really dense rock and is quite heavy. Some of the bigger rocks that I moved around the property were around 300-450lbs yes these were moved by hand. Only moving them via wheelbarrow when they neeed to be moved across the yard.

The Gardening

Gardening had become a staple around my house, when my sibling and I were younger, our mother would alway encourage/ guilt trip us into helping her with the gardening. when I got older I really started to enjoy gardening and landscaping building things like rockeries, ponds and cultivating the land. Due to the natural shady area gardening was a challenge though we always had home grown tomatoes, (from the greehouse), potatoes, carrots, and beans.
